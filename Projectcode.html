Discover Digital Literacy: Your Guide to a Smarter Online World

Digital literacy is the skill of using technology with confidence, awareness, and safety. It helps you find reliable information, protect your personal data, and communicate responsibly online. With digital literacy, you can explore the internet wisely and make informed, safe choices every day.

Discover Cyber Safety: Your Shield in the Online World

Stay protected online with quick, smart, and easy-to-follow practices that help you browse safely, guard your information, and make responsible digital choices.

Enable Multi-Factor Protection

Secure accounts with an additional verification other than a password by turning on Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).

Keep Your Devices Secure

Keep your gadgets safe by installing antivirus software, enabling your firewall, and locking your device with strong passwords or biometrics.

Stay Alert With Emails and Links

Handle emails and links with caution. Avoid opening attachments from unknown senders and do not click suspicious links to prevent phishing or malware.

Review and Adjust Privacy Settings

Regularly check or change the privacy settings on social media and apps to control what information you share.

Create Strong and Unique Passwords

Use complex passwords with mixed characters and avoid using the same password across platforms.

Be Careful With Public Wi-Fi

Limit sensitive transactions on public networks or use a trustworthy VPN to stay protected.

Back Up Important Data

Regularly back up essential files on encrypted drives or trusted cloud services to prepare for data loss.

Limit Personal Information Online

Avoid sharing too much identifiable information online to reduce the chances of identity theft.

Monitor Your Digital Footprint

Review your online presence regularly to ensure no sensitive or unauthorized information is publicly accessible.

Guide Children and Teens Online

Teach young users how to identify safe and unsafe online interactions, and maintain open communication with them.

Welcome to the Glossary!

This glossary is designed to help you understand the key terms, concepts, and ideas that shape today’s online world. Whether you're new to digital literacy or simply refreshing your knowledge, this guide will support you in becoming a more informed, responsible, and confident digital user.

Antivirus Software

Programs designed to detect, prevent, and remove harmful software from devices.

Backups

Copies of important data stored separately to prevent loss caused by device failure, hacking, or accidental deletion.

Cloud Storage

Online services that securely store digital files and allow access from any device with internet connection.

Credible Source

A trustworthy origin of information with identifiable authors, dates, and evidence.

Cyber Safety

Practices that protect users from online risks such as scams, malware, or identity theft.

Digital Etiquette (Netiquette)

Proper and respectful online behavior, including giving credit to creators and communicating responsibly.

Digital Footprint

The trail of online activities—such as posts, searches, and interactions—that represent a person’s online presence.

Digital Literacy

The ability to use technology confidently, responsibly, and safely while understanding how to find reliable information and protect personal data.

Digital Trends

New technologies, tools, or online behaviors that influence how people use the internet.

Digital Well-Being

Maintaining a healthy balance in online activities through screen-time control, breaks, and mindful device use.

Fact-Checking

The act of verifying information using multiple trusted sources before believing or sharing it.

File Management

Organizing digital files into folders, drives, or cloud storage for easier access and safety.

Firewall

A security system that blocks unauthorized access to a device or network.

Identity Theft

A crime where someone uses another person’s personal information to impersonate them online.

Malware

Harmful software designed to damage devices, steal data, or gain unauthorized access.

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

A security feature requiring two or more verification steps—like passwords and OTP codes—to access an account.

Password Strength

How secure a password is, based on its length and the mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.

Phishing

A scam where attackers pretend to be trusted sources to steal information or trick users into clicking dangerous links.

VPN (Virtual Private Network)

A tool that encrypts your internet connection, offering extra security—especially on public Wi-Fi.
